The Monk Diamond, a highly-prized jewel, was discovered in 1880 and initially purchased by a Russian nobleman for his wife in 1889.
It is over one billion years old, and its 300 carats make it worth more than Â£10 million.

During the Russian Revolution, the Monk Diamond was stolen from the nobleman's palace in St Petersburg. For the next thirty years, the Monk Diamond's whereabouts was unknown. In 1947 it was discovered during a police raid on a gang of petty criminals in Moscow. It was then returned to the nobleman's family

Deciding that the jewel was perhaps a bad omen, the nobleman's son sold the Monk Diamond to the House of Volkov, Moscow's oldest jewellery house. The House of Volkov paid an undisclosed sum for the jewel, but it was rumoured to be the most expensive diamond sale ever.

The Monk Diamond was on display in the House of Volkov's private collection until three years ago, when it was stolen in an audacious raid. Despite a long investigation and the promise of a huge reward, the police were unable to track down the culprits and the case has gone unsolved until now.
